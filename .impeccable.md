## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience: women ages 40–60, booking solo or with friends (bachelorettes, friend outings, date nights). They're browsing from their phone or laptop during downtime — evenings, weekends, a lunch break. The job to be done is simple: find a fun thing to do, feel good about booking it, show up and enjoy themselves. They want to feel like this is *their* kind of place before they've even bought a ticket.

### Brand Personality
**3 words: playful / social / unpretentious**

Bloom Babe is the friend who always knows where the fun is — not precious, not performative, just genuinely warm and a little bit cheeky. The copy ("get your hands dirty," "laugh with strangers") is the right register. The design should match: loose where it can be, intentional where it counts.

Emotional goals: delight on first look, comfort when deciding, excitement at checkout.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Vibe**: A well-loved independent boutique — the kind of place with good lighting, fresh flowers on the counter, and a hand-lettered chalkboard menu. Warm, tactile, slightly editorial. Not polished to the point of feeling expensive or exclusive. Not craft-fair scrappy either.

**Anti-references**: Kids' craft fair (too primary-color, too chaotic), corporate event company (too sterile, too formal), Pinterest-perfect floral brand (too curated, too cold).

**Theme**: Light — this is daytime, weekend energy. Browsed with coffee, on a couch.

**Color**: The existing cream/charcoal/gold palette is directionally right but not locked in. Open to evolution — the key is warmth and a little personality. Avoid anything that reads clinical or cool-toned.

**Typography**: Needs work. Playfair Display is an obvious AI-serif reflex; Inter is an overused body font. Find something with more character — something that feels like it was chosen on purpose, not defaulted to. The body font in particular should feel warm and approachable, not corporate-neutral.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over polish** — rough edges are fine; coldness is not. Every surface should feel like it was touched by a human.
2. **Social proof over selling** — the vibe sells itself. Lead with experience, not features or specs.
3. **Friction-free decisions** — clear hierarchy, obvious next steps. Women in their 40s–60s aren't here to decipher a clever UI.
4. **Earned delight** — small moments of joy (a hover, a transition, a detail) that reward attention without demanding it.
5. **Left-aligned where possible** — centered layouts are the easy default; asymmetric compositions feel more considered and editorial.
